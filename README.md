# Salary Hike Trend Analysis - Raddison Hospitality Group (2018-2022)

---

## About the Project

- Conducted a 5-year salary hike analysis (2018–2022) for Raddison Hospitality Group.
- Analyzed compensation trends across designations, gender and locations.
- Evaluated gender pay equity and attrition patterns to identify compensation-related risks.
- Developed an interactive dashboard to support HR decision-making and workforce planning.

---

## Key Metrics Analyzed

- Average Salary
- Year-over-Year (YoY) Salary Hike %
- Gender Pay Gap (GPG)
- Designation-wise Compensation Trends
- Location-wise Salary Trends
- Attrition Patterns and Drivers

## Key Insights

---

## Designation-wise Analysis

- Waiters & Housekeeping received the highest salary growth (150%) despite having the lowest salaries.
- Restaurant Supervisors recorded the second-highest salary growth (122%).
- Managers experienced a total salary increase of 64%.
- Chefs had the highest salary levels but the lowest salary growth (17%), indicating potential retention risk.
- Significant salary corrections were observed for lower-paid frontline roles.

<img width="890" height="711" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/64cf2482-2923-43d6-b406-ae171cc9ba21" />

## Gender-wise Analysis

- Male employees consistently received higher annual salary hikes than female employees.
- Restaurant Supervisors moved from a -56.4% pay gap in 2018 to near parity at 1.7% in 2022.
- Managers emerged as the most concerning group, with the widest gender pay gap of 14.5% by 2022.
- Female Chefs received significantly lower salary growth than male Chefs, contributing to a widening pay gap.

## Gender Pay Gap Findings

- Female employees generally earned more than male employees in 2018, but the gap narrowed over time.
- Organization-wide gender pay gap improved from approximately -53.3% in 2018 to -15.3% in 2022.
- Restaurant Supervisors achieved near pay parity by 2022.
- Managers recorded the highest positive gender pay gap in 2022, requiring immediate attention.

## Location-wise Analysis

- Shillong displayed the most irregular compensation patterns.
- Managers in Shillong received a 387% salary hike in 2021 followed by only 2% growth in 2022.
- Salary reductions were observed for some Shillong roles, highlighting compensation inconsistencies.
- Jaipur maintained relatively stable salary growth despite lower salary levels.
- Pune implemented substantial salary corrections for Waiters & Housekeeping in 2022.

## Attrition Insights

- Compensation emerged as the leading driver of employee attrition.
- Most attrition occurred among Waiters & Housekeeping employees.
- Male employees accounted for nearly 70% of attrition cases.
- Attrition was highest among employees with 7–10 years of experience.
- Frontline operational roles experienced the greatest turnover risk.
- Attrition peaked during April and December.

## Recommendations

- Implement structured salary bands and compensation frameworks.
- Conduct external salary benchmarking for frontline positions.
- Introduce retention bonuses for high-turnover roles.
- Establish transparent career progression pathways.
- Strengthen role enrichment and promotion programs.
- Address gender pay gaps across locations and designations.
- Review compensation practices in Shillong due to inconsistent salary patterns.
